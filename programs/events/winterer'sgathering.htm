Featured Speakers
 On September 23, 2015 Dave and Amy Freeman embarked on a yearlong adventure in the Boundary Waters Canoe Area Wilderness to share this national treasure with the world and help insure that the Boundary Waters remains protected for future generations to enjoy. They expected to travel more than 3,000 miles by canoe and dog team as they explored more than 500 lakes and streams throughout the year. They shared their adventures through regular blog posts, their websites and social media.

Event Highlights
Arctic Films Festival
 Celebrate Arctic culture and adventure at the Arctic Films Festival. Up to 16 films will cover the Arctic environment, history, northern expeditions, shelter, clothing and food. If you have an appropriate film in mind, please nominate it.

Harbor Side Winter Tent Camp
 Open to participants with the gear and fortitude to camp outdoors, the Harbor Side Tent Camp is adjacent to the North House campus in the Grand Marais Rec Park. Bring your canvas wall tent, traditional yurt, or expedition tent and share knowledge and ideas with other gatherers. Register for winter camping with North House, but remember to check in with the Rec Park.

Cold Snap Poetry Slam
 Come one, come all, to the first literary event of its kind at Winterers Gathering. Cold Snap Poetry Slam is a friendly celebration of the spoken word and the glories of the frozen north. Presenters receive a free bottomless bag of popcorn for the weekend, and are asked to sign up in advance.

Also featuring the Gear & Ski Swap, Snowshoe Shuffle Dance, Winter Skills Seminars, and Deep Freeze Chili Feed
