GROUP LEARNING

North House Folk School designs custom programs for groups in every month of the year.  Whether it's a simple evening slide show on campus or a multi-day workshop complete with abundant woodchips, the opportunity exists here to explore the world of traditional craft while simultaneously building your community.

All groups are welcome – whether a school group, college course, alumni organization, extended family, scout troop or tour group. The Group Flier (included below) lists learning experiences and serves as a useful guide, helping you shape your group learning experience on the North House Folk School campus.

Group Program Flier
North House works with group leaders to shape unique learning experiences that reflect the interests and abilities of the participants. Reviewing the group flier is always a helpful first step. Specific group enrollment and participation guidelines define the basic process of working together.

Longer Programs
North House has worked with several institutions hosting longer group learning experiences, including St Olaf College, YMCA Camp Menogyn, Cook County High School, Silver Bay High School and Wilderness Canoe Base.  Projects have focused on building timber frame structures and traditional wooden boats.  Other project ideas for extended group experiences are welcome.

Follow this link to learn more about North House's Timber Frame - Group Building projects. 

Scheduling
Early inquiries and flexibility of scheduling will maximize the range of opportunities available for each group. Availability of topics and dates is a function of classroom and instructor availability.  Questions and inquires are welcome throughout the year at 218-387-9762 or info@northhouse.org
